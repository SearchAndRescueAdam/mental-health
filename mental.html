Mission:
In an effort to combat mental illness on college campuses, we strive to inform students about mental illnesses. By recognizing and identifying ways to offer support to people in need, we can lower the stigma regarding mental health and encourage all to seek professional help.

What is mental health?

Mental Health Disorders, refers to a wide range of mental health conditions, these disorders can affect a person's mood, think and behavior. Such mental illness would be anxiety, depression, eating disorders and addictive behaviors. It's Normal for people to have mental health concerns from time to time. But it becomes concerning a mental illness when the same ongoing signs affect your ability to function in the community.
“Mental Illness.” Mayo Clinic, Mayo Foundation for Medical Education and Research, 8 June 2019, www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/mental-illness/symptoms-causes/syc-20374968.

What is anxiety?

You might feel anxious when faced with a stressful problem at work, before taking a huge test, or before making an important life decision.More serious than this Anxiety has the power to take away a person's life , and drive a person to the edge making it unbearable to live. What would be the risk factor in having Anxiety? Well here are some examples of Anxiety can cause given by the National Institute of Mental Health.

  • Feeling restless, wound-up, or on-edge
  • Being easily fatigued
  • Having difficulty concentrating, Mind going blank, Being irritable
  • Having muscle tension
  • Difficulty controlling feelings of worry
  • Having sleep problems, such as difficulty falling or staying asleep, restlessness, or unsatisfying sleep

What is depression?

A serious mood disorder that affects how you feel, think and handle daily activities, such as sleeping, eating, or working. Any form of depression that lasts longer than other series of two weeks would be the person suffering from deep depression. Depression can happen at any age that affects its host with loss of interest and decreased energy and fatigue. “Search.” National Institute of Mental Health, U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, www.nimh.nih.gov/search?q=depression%2B.

How to help

Common Signs

Anxiety

  • Frequent headaches
  • Nausea
  • Twitches or tremors
  • Excessive sweating
  • Restlessness

Depression

  • Changes in appetite
  • Lack of energy
  • Loss of interest in regular activities
  • Irritable
  • Tearfulness
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Dramatic changes in sleeping habits (insomnia/sleeping all the time)
  • Frequent/reoccurring thoughts of suicide

DO: Validate and take their feelings seriously, let them talk, commiserate, but don’t say that you “understand”, let them know about other support services (counseling groups, doctors, support positive dialogue of medication, respond to emergencies, take care of yourself (make sure that you have someone you can talk to, don’t try to fix every problem). Stay aware of resources in your area so you can share information

DON'T: tell them to “snap out of it”, encourage unhealthy coping habits (drugs/alcohol), avoid them after they open up to you, judge or criticise, assume the problem will resolve itself, imply that they are a burden *always ask permission to contact help on behalf of a friend or peer, unless you fear that they may be in immediate danger
